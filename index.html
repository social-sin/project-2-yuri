The Series Biggest Leap Forward

The 2008 model of Mario Kart introduces a refined aesthetic and an array of innovative features, effectively utilizing the Wii hardware. Enhancements include improved track details, advanced character animations, and enhanced lighting effects. The revamped, minimalist white menus add to the Wii's distinct atmosphere.

Unlike its predecessor, Double Dash!, which featured two-character racing teams, MK Wii allows players to choose between karts and bikes, increasing the number of racers to 12. This escalation heightens the competitive tension, especially in the 150cc class. Additionally, racers can now perform midair tricks that yield a Mini-Turbo boost upon landing, adding a new layer of strategy to gameplay.

New Features

Nearly all classic items from the series have returned, closely mirroring the arsenal of the most recent installment. Additionally, three new items have been introduced: the Mega Mushroom from New Super Mario Bros. (which allows players to drive over others as a giant), the classic Mario Bros. POW Block (which jolts all other cars to a stop after a warning), and the Lightning Cloud (which grants a speed boost for a short time before shrinking the user, allowing gamers to pass on the effect by bumping into others). These changes contribute to a gameplay experience that, while still distinctly Mario Kart, offers the freshest shell-slinging action since the original SNES title was released over 15 years ago.

Mario Kart Wii prominently features the Nintendo Wii Wheel, which is integral to the gameplay experience and packaged with the game. While the game also supports other controllers, the developers specifically designed it with the Wii Wheel in mind. Players transitioning from previous titles like Double Dash! and Mario Kart DS may need to adapt, as the wheel presents a different steering style that involves less physical movement for turns. Initially, players may oversteer, but with practice, they can effectively control their racers by making minor adjustments. The Wii Wheel creates an immersive arcade-like experience without the cumbersome nature of traditional gaming wheels, and it can also be used with just the Wii remote.

Top Tracks

Mario Kart Wii has an impressive lineup of 32 racetracks, including exciting new tracks and updated classic favorites; here's a sneak peek at some of the standouts.

Mario Circuit

Flower Cup

A short track with a great straightaway for wheelies, a testy Chain Chomp, and a very long drift to the finish line.

DS Yoshi Falls

Shell Cup

The exact same as on the DS, only the second bridge is now a great spot to catch some air and trick for a mini-turbo.

DS Delfino Square

Banana Cup

Drifting around the fountain quickly charges a stage 2 mini turbo, and the drawbridge is great for big-air tricks.
